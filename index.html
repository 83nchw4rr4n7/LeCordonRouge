Persecution 2.0

The internet is one of the most powerful tools ever wielded by mankind. It's potential has not gone unnoticed. Already The People's Republic of China and more recently Syria have used the internet to hunt down dissenting voices leading to imprisonment and execution.

Persecution is as old as the church. The methods however are advancing daily. Worse still, we have let those technologies go unchallenged due to ignorance or apathy. The internet is the new global commons. We must not yield this ground. If it was a physical location, we would have little pause in deploying traditional strategies such as sending missionaries or Bible smuggling. Our new Digital Age presents the church with new challenges and oppurtunities demanding new perspectives and new strategies. This is what Le Cordon Rouge hopes to provide.

We are attempting to create a new form of ministry or outreach to combat modern persecution. We will focus on four major areas:

Distribution

  • Make anti-censorship and security software more available.
  • Provide training in the use of those tools online at this site, via streaming media (YouTube), and by filesharing networks (torrent) as well as offline in person demonstrations.
  • Contribute to the OpenSource communities that have developed these tools.

Intelligence

  • Gather data from people in persecuted nations.
  • Consult IT and security proffesionals.
  • Develop sets of "Best Practices" based on our gathered intel and peer review.

Awareness

  • Develop an awareness campaign that targets both secular and religious audiences.
  • Produce materials in a wide array of media types.
  • Lend support to like minded organizations.

Networking

  • Engage both faith based and secular activists in persecuted nations to at least share news and possibly coordinate actions.
  • Have a physical presence at events related to our work. This would include faith based events or secular events such as Defcon, a hacker convention, or Amnesty Internationals General Meeting.
